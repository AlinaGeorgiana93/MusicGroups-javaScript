Music, a universal form of art, dates back to prehistoric times when early humans used primitive instruments and their voices to create rhythms and sounds for communication, and entertainment. Over millennia, music evolved through various cultures and civilizations, with notable contributions from ancient Greece, India, and China, where it played a central role in ceremonies and storytelling. During the Middle Ages in Europe, Gregorian chants and religious music dominated, eventually giving way to the rich, polyphonic textures of the Renaissance. The Baroque period introduced complex compositions and orchestras, epitomized by composers like Bach and Handel. The Classical era, led by Mozart and Beethoven, emphasized clarity, balance, and form, laying the groundwork for Romantic music's emotional expressiveness in the 19th century. The 20th century saw an explosion of musical diversity, from jazz and blues to rock and electronic music, driven by technological advancements and global cultural exchange. Today, music continues to evolve, blending traditional elements with modern innovations, reflecting the ever-changing human experience.
